## Student Feedback Summary – AI Tutor Survey

**Dataset**: 47 consented responses with non-empty free-response answers (Q7).  
**Goal**: Categorize key themes in students’ qualitative feedback and count how many students mentioned each theme.

### Overall Themes (counts out of 47)

- **Course-specific focus** (`theme_course_specific`) – **12 students (25.5%)**  
  Students highlighted that the AI Tutor is tightly aligned with course materials (lecture slides/notes), which they appreciated for staying on-topic and avoiding hallucinations.

- **General positive sentiment** (`theme_general_positive`) – **10 students (21.3%)**  
  Comments praise the idea of the AI Tutor, its usefulness as a resource, and its interface/usability. Some say they plan to use it more (e.g., for the final) because it seems promising and easy to use.

- **Did not use / forgot to use** (`theme_did_not_use`) – **9 students (19.1%)**  
  A noticeable group report barely or never using the tutor, often because they forgot about it, did not know where to find it, or felt they had sufficient alternative resources (slides, other tools).

- **Comparison to other chatbots** (`theme_other_chatbots_better`) – **8 students (17.0%)**  
  Many students compare the AI Tutor to tools like ChatGPT. They often say the Tutor is better for class-specific, accurate information, but public chatbots can be more flexible, provide analogies, and support broader questions.

- **Exam / study support** (`theme_exam_helpful`) – **7 students (14.9%)**  
  Several students mention using or planning to use the Tutor for midterm/final preparation. They describe it as helpful for reviewing material and studying, though sometimes limited in depth.

- **Too restricted / narrow** (`theme_too_restricted`) – **5 students (10.6%)**  
  These students feel the Tutor is overly constrained by its limited training data. They say it often just points them to slides or sources without deeper processing or explanations, which undermines its value as a “tutor.”

- **UI or access issues** (`theme_ui_or_access_issues`) – **3 students (6.4%)**  
  A few students struggled to find the chatbot link, experienced it not working at times, or found some response formatting hard to read. These issues reduced how often they used it.

- **Not capable enough** (`theme_not_capable_enough`) – **3 students (6.4%)**  
  Students here describe the Tutor as not doing what they asked, producing overly basic questions, or failing to generate good practice exams or walkthroughs. They wanted more sophisticated, customized, and guided practice.

- **Points to slides only** (`theme_points_to_slides_only`) – **2 students (4.3%)**  
  Some explicitly say the Tutor often just points them to where information is in the slides, rather than synthesizing or explaining content. They perceive it more as a “search tool” than an intelligent tutor.

- **Call limits** (`theme_call_limits`) – **1 student (2.1%)**  
  At least one student mentions the limited number of allowed calls as a deterrent, causing them to conserve usage and ultimately use the Tutor less.

### High-level Takeaways

- **Strengths**: Strong alignment with course materials, perceived accuracy on class-specific content, and generally positive reactions to the idea and interface of the AI Tutor. It is particularly valued as a focused study tool for the course.
- **Weaknesses**: Perceived over-restriction and limited capabilities (often just linking to slides rather than teaching), plus usability and discoverability issues that reduce actual usage.
- **Behavioral pattern**: A meaningful subset of students either **did not use** or **rarely used** the Tutor, sometimes because they preferred other tools, sometimes because of access/awareness issues, and sometimes because they perceived limited added value beyond existing course resources.
